New: two ways to bring Obsidian to your whole company

Real two-way vault sync new

Point Ledger at an actual Obsidian vault folder on disk and it syncs both directions, file by file. Edit a note in Obsidian, edit the same page in Ledger, and they converge. It's conflict-aware and never loses an edit: when both sides change a file, Obsidian (the file) wins and your prior Ledger version is kept in version history to diff and restore. New notes create on the other side; an edit always beats a delete. Optional auto-sync every 30 seconds and on focus.

Uses the browser's File System Access API in Chromium-class browsers (Chrome, Edge, Brave, Arc). On Firefox/Safari, use the lossless Import/Export round-trip. How sync works →

The enterprise layer Obsidian doesn't have

Obsidian is brilliant for a single person. The moment a company depends on those notes, you need governance Obsidian was never built to give. That layer is the product.

Shared org vault + roles

One company vault with real roles & permissions — who can read, edit, approve, or publish each space. Admin, Editor, Reviewer, Viewer, Portal. Obsidian has no concept of who's allowed to touch what.

Review & approval governance

Every page carries an owner and a last-reviewed date. Ledger flags stale docs, records sign-off, and an edit re-opens approval. Documentation auditors will actually trust.

Read-only published portals

Share a space with non-editors and auditors as a self-contained read-only portal — they read, they can't change a thing, and they need no account.

Org-wide search + links graph

Search-as-you-type across every space — titles, tags, frontmatter, body. Plus a links/graph view: backlinks and "what depends on this?", the connection map Obsidian draws, made navigable.

Audit trail & SSO

The cloud tier adds an append-only audit trail and single sign-on (one login across the suite) — the controls a company needs and a personal vault can't provide.

Still no secret storage

Ledger holds zero passwords or secrets. Credential pages link to your password manager by item URL. A breach of Ledger leaks no credentials, because there are none to leak.

The Obsidian → company-grade flow

  1. Connect your vault — point Ledger at a real Obsidian vault folder for live two-way sync, or paste/import a bundle. Frontmatter, folders, tags and [[wikilinks]] carry over. (A sample KB is preloaded to explore.)
  2. Sign in — a first-class Sign in button in the top bar adds the shared org vault; signed-out stays fully local and makes zero network calls.
  3. Browse spaces on the left rail — the "spine" — grouped by folder, with a tag cloud and org-wide search.
  4. Open a page — rendered Markdown, a frontmatter table, wiki-links, backlinks, and a Links view showing what depends on it.
  5. Govern it — see the owner and last-reviewed status; mark reviewed today; a Reviewer approves the version.
  6. Switch roles (Admin / Editor / Reviewer / Viewer / Portal) to see exactly what each can do — edit, approve, publish, or read-only.
  7. Publish a space as a read-only portal — one self-contained HTML file an auditor opens with no account, no editing.
  8. Export any page or the whole vault as portable Markdown — open it back in Obsidian. No lock-in by design.
