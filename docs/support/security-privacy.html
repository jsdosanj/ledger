Security & privacy

Ledger Governance runs entirely inside Obsidian, on your own machine. There is no account, no token, no server, and no network call. Your notes stay portable plain Markdown, and everything the plugin records is written into files you already own.

The short version: nothing is sent anywhere. Ledger reads and writes Markdown files in your vault and stores its settings in your vault's local plugin data. That is the whole footprint.

100% local — no network calls

Ledger makes no network requests. It does not call home, check for licenses, fetch remote content, or report usage. There is no account to create, no token to paste, and no telemetry of any kind. You can confirm this for yourself: the full source is on GitHub, and the plugin behaves identically with networking disabled.

  • No cloud, no sync service. Ledger never uploads or downloads your notes. It is not a hosted product and has no companion web service.
  • No background processes. It only does work when you run one of its commands or open the dashboard.

Where your data lives

Everything Ledger touches stays in your Obsidian vault, in plain text you can read, edit, and version-control yourself.

Governance metadata: your notes' frontmatter

Governance lives in standard YAML frontmatter at the top of each note — fields like title, owner, status, type, reviewed_on, reviewer, and review_cadence_days. Because it is ordinary frontmatter, your notes remain portable plain Markdown that reads fine with or without the plugin installed.

The audit log: _ledger/audit.md

Every governance action appends a row to _ledger/audit.md — a plain Markdown table (date, action, document, detail) inside your vault. You own that file. It never leaves your machine, and you can read, edit, archive, or delete it like any other note.

Settings: local plugin data

Your settings — the review-stale threshold, "Require an owner," and "Require a status" — are stored in the vault's local plugin data folder (.obsidian/plugins/ledger-governance/). They travel with your vault and nowhere else.

No secrets, no passwords

Ledger stores no passwords and no secrets. It records governance metadata only — who owns a document, its status, and when it was last reviewed. If you keep credential notes in your vault, link them to an item in your password manager rather than pasting the secret into the note. Ledger has no feature that reads, scans, or stores secret values.

Free and open source

Ledger Governance is free and licensed under the permissive MIT License. You can read every line, audit exactly what it does, fork it, or contribute. There is no paid tier and nothing is gated.

A governance aid — not a backup or a security control. Ledger helps you track ownership, status, and review freshness across your own notes. It is not a substitute for your own backups, your password manager, or your organization's security and compliance controls.
